Second Blog: The URL

Credit: Image from https://www.successcds.net/full-form/url-full-form-what-is-the-full-form-of-url.html

The URL, according to the MDN website, stands for Uniform Resource Locator, it is an address of a given unique resource on the Web. The URL is a tool used by browsers to extract any published resource on the web. Browsers are programs that retrieve and display pages from the web, and let users access further pages through hyperlinks; hyperlinks connect webpages or data items to one another. The URL is composed of different parts, some parts of it are important and some are optional.

Fourth Blog: Hypertext

Credit: Image from https://www.flickr.com/photos/natematias/33621866

The meaning of hypertext is one that was very difficult to define, many researchers and scholars have tried to define it, however it seems as though overtime someone comes up with a definition, there are always people waiting to disagree with them there is always fault with someone’s definition and as a result. People came up with their own definition anyway of what a hypertext is. Marie-LaureRyan[5] stated that in “in hypertext, the reader determines the unfolding of the text clicking on hyperlinks that bring to the screen other segments of a text”. Espen Aarseth[1] argued that hypertext is merely a direct link from one position in a text to another; well known known researchers argued against this definition because they believed that link-based understandings missed “the profound aspects of hypertext” especially “hypertext-as-interaction with information to build associations, and through associations build knowledge”.
