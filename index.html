  • First Blog: The History of The Internet

    In this article I will be discussing the interesting points that I picked up from the three readings that I read this week. The first reading that I will be discussing is an essay titled “As We May Think” written by Vannevar Bush in the year 1945. The second will be a short story titled “The Library of Babel”, which was written by Jorge Luis Borges in the year 1998, Bush wrote about his beliefs and his hopes for the advancement of technological devices in the future. Bush believed that machines and technological/digital innovations could be made and improved with great economic effort; this gives off the impression that Bush believed that capitalism was the driver of innovation. He used the example of Liebniz, who invented a calculating machine called the Step Reckoner; Liebniz’s calculating machine was not and could not developed for commercial production, and Bush believed that this was because the “economics of the situation” were against Liebniz and his invention(); he argued that the labour involved in constructing the machine “exceeded the labour to be saved by its use”(Bush V, 1979, pg37) , calculating using a piece of paper and a pen seemed easier.

  • Second Blog: The URL



    The URL, according to the MDN website, stands for Uniform Resource Locator, it is an address of a given unique resource on the Web. The URL is a tool used by browsers to extract any published resource on the web. Browsers are programs that retrieve and display pages from the web, and let users access further pages through hyperlinks; hyperlinks connect webpages or data items to one another.
